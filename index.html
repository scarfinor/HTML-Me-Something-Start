How To Build A Retaining Wall System

Materials

Block Types:

Garden wall, Retaining wall, Cap Stone

Fill:

1 Inch Clean, Top Soil, Decretive Rock, Mulch

Fabric:

Filter Fabric, Weed Barrier

Adhesive:

LP 300 Landscape Adhesive

1.Design

People build retaining walls for a variety of different reasons. The first and probably most important reason for building a retaining wall in you yard is to combat negative slope towards your homes foundation. The second reason is to hide foundation walls that otherwise would be exposed. The third reason someone would build a retaining wall system is a raised garden bed. It is important to think about what you want you retaining wall to do when choosing the shape size or color of your wall. There are many different variations of size and color block that you can choose from. The most common block size is 12x18x6. It give you a strong foot print to insure your retaining wall system will not move.

2.Base

The first step in building a retaining wall system is to dig a trench big enough and deep enough for you your materials to be encapsulated. standard convention is to dig a 12x12 trench for the total length of your wall. Once the Trench is dug you can begin to fill the trench with a strong base material like inch minus or 1 inch clean crushed crock. The next step is to compact the rock down so that it will not move after this step it is time to start setting blocks into the rock base.

3.Stacking

After you have laid a sturdy and level base you can begin stacking blocks on top of your base blocks. It is important to start your next course on top of the center of two base blocks. Once your course is set you need to secure them down with a interlocking pin system. At this point your retaining wall system should be taking shape and ready to fill.

4.Back Fill

Now that your wall is stacked to height you can begin the back filling process. This is where you will take raw materials like dirt or rock and fill the empty space behind the block. this step will be the body of your project. If you are planing to have a strong structure to walk or drive on top of it is a good idea to fill with rock. If you are using the retaining wall system as a raised garden bed you can just use top soil for planting soil.

Cap

Cap is the final step in building a good retaining wall system. Cap is a slightly smaller version of the block you used to base and stack your wall that has a smooth top. It is used to cover any gaps or holes from the top of the wall giving it a polished finishing look. The cap are installed by a flush cut method and then glued in place on top of your stacking blocks.
